Understanding TWO’s Business Model

As a mortgage REIT, Two Harbors invests primarily in residential mortgage-backed securities (RMBS) and related real estate debt instruments. This structure allows the firm to generate revenue mainly through net interest income and servicing fees. Investors benefit from steady dividend streams, typically higher than many traditional equities, making it appealing in a low-yield environment.

However, the performance of mortgage REITs like TWO is closely tied to interest rates, prepayment speeds, and credit risk in mortgage markets. This creates potential for both attractive returns and increased vulnerability depending on macroeconomic trends.
