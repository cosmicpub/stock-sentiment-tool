FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common questions about Stocksentimentscore.com, its sentiment score, and how to use the results responsibly.

What does Stocksentimentscore.com do?

Stocksentimentscore.com analyzes recent headlines connected to a stock and estimates whether the overall news tone appears bullish, bearish, or mixed. It also highlights the stronger matching headlines and the main drivers behind the result.

Is the sentiment score a price prediction?

No. The score is not a guaranteed prediction of future stock movement. It is a summary of recent news pressure based on the headlines the tool found and ranked as relevant.

What does confidence mean?

Confidence reflects how convincing and consistent the current headline signal appears to be. Higher confidence usually means multiple relevant stories are pointing in a similar direction. Lower confidence usually means the signal is weaker, thinner, or mixed.

Why are some headlines included while others are ignored?

Not every article mentioning a company is equally useful. Ticker Checker tries to reduce noise by ranking headlines for relevance and filtering out weaker, overly generic, or less meaningful stories when possible.

Can I use this as financial advice?

No. Stocksentimentscore.com is for informational and educational purposes only. It should be used alongside broader research, not as a substitute for full investment analysis or professional advice.

Why does a stock sometimes rise even if sentiment looks bearish?

Stock prices move for many reasons. Market expectations, earnings quality, guidance, macroeconomic data, technical setups, and broader investor positioning can all affect price. News sentiment is only one piece of the puzzle.
