Understanding Two Harbors’ Business Model

Two Harbors primarily invests in residential mortgage-backed securities (RMBS), capitalizing on spread income between borrowing costs and asset yields. As an mREIT, it uses leverage to amplify returns, which can boost income but also introduces risk, especially in volatile interest rate environments.

Its portfolio combines agency RMBS—which carry government guarantees and lower risk—and non-agency RMBS, which expose the company to credit risk. Managing this balance is key to steady earnings.

Market Conditions Shaping TWO's Outlook
