Utility Industry Context and Hawaiian Electric's Position

The utility sector faces enormous shifts, primarily driven by the energy transition to renewables and regulatory pressures to reduce carbon footprints. Hawaiian Electric is uniquely challenged and positioned as an island utility that must deeply integrate solar, wind, and battery storage technologies while maintaining grid stability across dispersed populations.

Unlike mainland utilities, HE cannot rely on neighboring grids for backup or efficient generation sharing, meaning infrastructure investments tend to be more expensive and complex. This factor restricts margin expansion and limits earnings volatility.
