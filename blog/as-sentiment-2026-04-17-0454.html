Industry and macro context

The textiles, apparel, and luxury goods industry is highly sensitive to global economic conditions, consumer sentiment, raw material prices, and supply chain stability.

The Iran war has intensified challenges in 2026, evidenced by several Reuters headlines covering the broader fallout including strained aviation sectors and energy market disruptions in Asia and Europe. Industrial and consumer sectors face costs pressures and demand shocks in key regions.

Additionally, the IMF warns of uneven fallout from the conflict across Middle East states, exacerbating trade and investment uncertainty. Rising defense spending worldwide adds to the “guns versus butter” dilemma, potentially limiting government social spending that supports economic growth.

The reduction in German GDP forecasts to a mere 0.5% reflects the risk of stagnation in Europe's largest economy which is an important market and supply hub for luxury apparel brands like Amer Sports.
