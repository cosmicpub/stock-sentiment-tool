Why ARE’s Specialized Real Estate Model Matters

Unlike traditional real estate investment trusts (REITs) that focus on offices or residential properties, ARE targets a niche but rapidly expanding space: real estate for the life science and technology sectors. This includes laboratories, research facilities, and innovation hubs primarily located in high-barrier-to-entry markets such as Boston, San Francisco, and San Diego.

This specialization offers two main advantages. First, life science tenants often sign long-term, triple-net leases, which provide steady, predictable income streams. Second, demand for such properties is becoming increasingly inelastic given the growth in biotech innovation, government healthcare focus, and the ongoing need for cutting-edge R&D infrastructure.
