Understanding TWO’s Core Business Model

Two Harbors focuses primarily on agency mortgage-backed securities and other mortgage-related assets, benefiting from spread income — the difference between borrowing costs and returns on investments. This model typically thrives when interest rates stabilize or decline. However, rising rates can compress spreads and hurt earnings.

The appeal lies in consistent income generation via dividends, which has historically drawn investors seeking yield over growth. But that strategy exposes TWO to refinancing risks and mortgage prepayment volatility, making the stock’s performance sensitive to economic cycles.
